Pioneers of the Periodic Table

Johann Wolfgang Döbereiner
1829
Proposed the concept of triads — groups of three elements with similar properties and atomic weights.
Alexandre-Emile Béguyer de Chancourtois
1862
Created the first geometrical representation of the periodicity of elements using a helical graph (Telluric Helix).
John Newlands
1864
Proposed the Law of Octaves — elements show similar properties every eight elements when arranged by increasing atomic mass.
Lothar Meyer
1869
Independently developed a periodic table showing periodic trends, but published after Mendeleev.
Dmitri Mendeleev
1869
Created the first widely accepted periodic table. Arranged elements by atomic mass and predicted properties of undiscovered elements.
Henry Moseley
1913
Rearranged the periodic table by atomic number instead of atomic mass, resolving inconsistencies in Mendeleev's table.
Glenn T. Seaborg
1940
Discovered transuranium elements and proposed the actinide series, reshaping the bottom part of the periodic table.
